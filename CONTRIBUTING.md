# Contributing

If you've been there — somewhere outside your comfort zone, representing something you built, with the odds not exactly in your favor — you belong here.

## What's a dispatch

A dispatch is a firsthand account from a founder who's been somewhere. Not a travel blog post. Not a success story. A specific situation — written for the next founder about to face the same thing. Practical, honest, earned.

## The structure

Every dispatch has three sections. What you put inside them is yours.

- **Where you were, and why**: The context. City, event, what brought you there. Keep it short.

- **What you faced**: The situation. On the ground. What it actually felt like. Keep it short.

- **What to tell the next founder**: The useful part — the bus route that saved you $60, the neighborhood, the thing nobody told you until you were already there. The one thing you'd say if they called you the night before their flight.

Use the sections as anchors, not cages. Write a sentence or three paragraphs. Use a list if it's clearer. Include numbers if they help, a photo if it adds something, a mistake if it's the most honest part.

## Format

Write in Markdown. Start from the template in [`dispatches/TEMPLATE.md`](dispatches/TEMPLATE.md).

Length: as long as it needs to be, no longer.

## Naming

Name your file:

`city-event.md` — e.g. `buenos-aires-builderfest.md`

## How to submit

Drop your file in `/dispatches` and open a pull request.

## What we're looking for

We are not looking for success stories.
We are looking for honest learning.

Your dispatch doesn't need to be perfect. It needs to be honest.

# Dispatch Guide

A dispatch is a firsthand account from a founder who's been somewhere. Not a travel blog post. Not a success story. A specific situation — written for the next founder about to face the same thing.

## The structure

Every dispatch has four sections. What you put inside them is yours.

- **Where you were, and why**: The context. City, event, what brought you there.

- **What you faced**: The situation. On the ground. What it actually felt like.

- **What you figured out**: The useful part. The bus route that saved you $60. The neighborhood. The thing nobody told you until you were already there.

- **What to tell the next founder**: The one thing you'd say if they called you the night before their flight.

---

Use the sections as anchors, not cages. Write a sentence or three paragraphs. Use a list if it's clearer. Include numbers if they help, a photo if it adds something, a mistake if it's the most honest part.

## Format and naming

Write in Markdown. Name your file:

`YYYY-city-name.md` — e.g. `2025-buenos-aires-sebastian-pabon.md`

Length: as long as it needs to be, no longer.

## One last thing

Drop your file in `/dispatches` and open a pull request.

Your dispatch doesn't need to be perfect. It needs to be honest.
